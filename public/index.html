Warm-Up: Sharks vs Minnows

Everyone gets a ball and splits into age groups. In each group, one or two players are “Sharks” and the rest are “Minnows.” Minnows dribble to avoid the sharks, who try to kick their balls out. High-energy, competitive, and gets every player moving with the ball right from the start.

Key Focus
  • Ball at feet at all times — shield and protect
  • Quick change of direction and close dribble control
  • Split by age group for the right challenge level

Skill Stations — Rotate Every 10 Min

Players split into 3 small groups and rotate through 3 stations. Each group spends 10 minutes at every station, then rotates clockwise on the whistle. More touches, less standing in line, and every player works on every skill.

Station A

Partner Passing & Control

Pair up and face your partner. Strict 2-touch: first touch settles the ball, second touch passes it back. No holding. The rhythm and clean contact are everything.

Key Focus
  • Clean 1st touch — ball under control
  • Accurate 2nd touch back to partner
  • Eye contact and communication
Station B

Pass & Drive — On the Move

Pass, then immediately drive into space. Your partner controls and drives toward your run. The whole point: never stand still after releasing the ball.

Key Focus
  • Pass and move — never stand still
  • Drive with the ball at pace
  • Receive on the run
Station C

Shooting Mini Games

Receive a pass, drive at the goal, finish. Competitive and high-rep. Combines everything from Stations A and B and ends in a real shot.

Key Focus
  • Receive, settle, drive at goal
  • Clean technique — plant foot, follow through
  • Compete — keep score for game pressure
💧
Water Break — 2 Minutes
43–45 MIN  ·  Final break before scrimmage
45–60 MIN

Scrimmage

The final 15 minutes are pure scrimmage, split by age group. Players get to apply everything from the session — first touch, passing combinations, and driving with the ball — in a live game format. The emphasis is on reading the game, making decisions, and having fun competing.

Key Focus
  • Apply session skills: touch, passing, driving
  • Decision making — when to pass, when to drive
  • Compete, communicate, and have fun

FAQ

Questions parents ask

What ages is this for? ▼
Group classes are built for K–5 (ages 5–10). This is the technical foundation window before club soccer — kids who develop with qmtk in elementary school typically enter club at U10/U11 ahead of peers who started club at U6. Private training is open to all ages, K through high school.
Are sessions split by age? ▼
Yes. Elementary and Middle School groups run separately. A dedicated Kindergarten–1st grade group will be added depending on sign-up volume. Dedicated coaches for each group starting next season.
Does my child need prior experience? ▼
No. Beginners are absolutely welcome. qmtk is designed to build fundamentals from the ground up.
How do I know what's happening each week? ▼
Check this site — we post the full weekly session plan every week so you know exactly what's happening.
Are there tryouts? ▼
No tryouts. Ever. qmtk is open enrollment — any child in the age range can sign up. We believe elite coaching should be accessible to every kid.
How do I register? ▼
Group classes in Andover are registered through the Andover Recreation Department on MyRec. Click Enroll on this page. For private training, book through the link above or email noah@qmtk.org.
What should my child bring? ▼
Soccer cleats or athletic shoes, shin guards, a ball (extras available), and water. Dress for the weather — sessions run rain or shine unless fields are unsafe.
Do sessions include scrimmages? ▼
Yes. Every session ends with competitive scrimmage time — real game situations with goalies and proper goals. The best way to develop is to play.
Is qmtk coming to Reading? ▼
Yes. qmtk is actively working to launch sessions in Reading. Interested Reading parents or rec officials should reach out to noah@qmtk.org.
Where are classes held? ▼
Spring 2026 outdoor classes (Apr 27–Jun 12, Mon & Fri) are at Sanborn Elementary School — Lower Field, Andover. Private training locations are flexible. Reading-area sessions are coming soon.
How much does it cost? ▼
Outdoor spring 2026 group classes are $260 for Andover residents and $275 for non-residents — covering the full outdoor session (Apr 27–Jun 12, Mon & Fri 5:50–6:50 PM at Sanborn Elementary). Private training rates available upon inquiry.
