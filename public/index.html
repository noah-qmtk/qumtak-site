The Coach's Journey

From Arlington playgrounds to the German Bundesliga — and back.

Every drill, every session, every piece of feedback at Qumtak is built on a path no other local coach has walked.

Playing Career

Origin · 2000s–2010s
AC Arlington

The Beginning ▼

Shaya's father built AC Arlington from scratch — a handful of kids and an unshakeable belief in community soccer. Shaya was there from day one, helping shape the culture that made it legendary. The club grew to over 1,000 kids and became one of the most decorated programs in Massachusetts — winning the MA State Championship, the VW Tournament, and the MA Futsal State Championship (2009). That culture — where every kid wanted more — is the blueprint for Qumtak.

🏆 State Champs · VW Tournament · Futsal Champs 2009

Crossing the Atlantic ▼

The game took Shaya across the Atlantic to Mönchengladbach — suiting up for 1.FC Mönchengladbach and experiencing professional German football culture firsthand. He absorbed the tactical depth, discipline, and developmental philosophy that separates the German model from the American one. It was just the beginning of the European chapter.

🇩🇪 First German Club
03
Peak · 2015/16
Alemannia Klosterhardt
Oberhausen, Germany
Highest of Heights ▼

📍 Coach Shaya — back row, third from left

The journey reached its peak at Alemannia Klosterhardt — competing in the U19 Bundesliga West (A-Junioren), Germany's top-tier youth competition. Playing against the country's best young talent shaped Shaya's understanding of elite development, high-performance culture, and what it actually takes to build a world-class player. Every Qumtak session runs on that standard.

⭐ U19 Bundesliga West · 2015/16

The New Mission ▼

Everything came full circle. Shaya returned to Massachusetts with a professional data analytics career, a European playing pedigree, and the AC Arlington playbook in his DNA. Qumtak is the result — elite methodology, community-first values, no gatekeeping.


The vision: fill every local playground with technically excellent players. The US won't win a World Cup until we fix development at the grassroots. That starts here.
