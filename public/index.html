0–10 MIN

Warm-Up with the Ball

Players start with individual juggling to get comfortable on the ball, focusing on touch and control. After a few minutes, players pair up and begin passing with their partners to build rhythm and communication.

Key Focus
  • Solo juggling: ball mastery + first touch
  • Partner passing: accuracy and soft passes
  • Getting comfortable on an outdoor surface
10–15 MIN

Passing + Moving

Still in their pairs, players continue passing but now add movement after each pass. The goal is to train the habit of moving into space immediately after releasing the ball, a fundamental of real game play.

Key Focus
  • Pass and immediately move to new space
  • Receiving on the move
  • Field awareness and spacing
15–30 MIN

Agility + Ladder by Age Group

Players split into age groups to work the agility ladder. Younger players focus on basic footwork patterns and coordination. Older players work on speed, lateral movements, and explosive first steps. Each group rotates through the ladder, emphasizing proper technique.

Key Focus
  • Younger group: high knees, 1-2 step, balance
  • Older group: speed, lateral cuts, ickey shuffle
  • Proper footwork and body mechanics for all
30–60 MIN

Scrimmage + In-Game Drills

The final 30 minutes are split by age group. Younger players run a small-sided scrimmage focused on applying what they learned: spacing, first touch, and moving after the ball. Older players work through in-game drills and combination play before a full tactical scrimmage.

Key Focus
  • Younger: small-sided game, fun format, spacing
  • Older: 1v1s, combination play, tactical awareness
  • Applying all session skills in live game situations

FAQ

Questions parents ask

What ages is this for? ▼
Kindergarten through 8th grade. Elementary and Middle School groups run separately. A dedicated Kindergarten–1st grade group will be added depending on sign-up volume.
Are sessions split by age? ▼
Yes. Elementary and Middle School groups run separately. A dedicated Kindergarten–1st grade group will be added depending on sign-up volume. Dedicated coaches for each group starting next season.
Does my child need prior experience? ▼
No. Beginners are absolutely welcome. Qumtak is designed to build fundamentals from the ground up.
How do I know what's happening each week? ▼
Check this site — we post the full weekly session plan every week so you know exactly what's happening.
Are there tryouts? ▼
No tryouts. Ever. Qumtak is open enrollment — any child in the age range can sign up. We believe elite coaching should be accessible to every kid.
How do I register? ▼
Group classes in Andover are registered through the Andover Recreation Department on MyRec. Click Enroll on this page. For private training, book through the link above or email noah@qmtk.org.
What should my child bring? ▼
Soccer cleats or athletic shoes, shin guards, a ball (extras available), and water. Dress for the weather — sessions run rain or shine unless fields are unsafe.
Do sessions include scrimmages? ▼
Yes. Every session ends with competitive scrimmage time — real game situations with goalies and proper goals. The best way to develop is to play.
Is Qumtak coming to Reading? ▼
Yes. Qumtak is actively working to launch sessions in Reading. Interested Reading parents or rec officials should reach out to noah@qmtk.org.
Where are classes held? ▼
Spring 2026 outdoor classes (Apr 27–Jun 12, Mon & Fri) are at Sanborn Elementary School — Lower Field, Andover. Private training locations are flexible. Reading-area sessions are coming soon.
How much does it cost? ▼
Outdoor spring 2026 group classes are $260 for Andover residents and $275 for non-residents — covering the full outdoor session (Apr 27–Jun 12, Mon & Fri 5:50–6:50 PM at Sanborn Elementary). Private training rates available upon inquiry.
