What a Session Looks Like

Every hour is planned. Every minute counts.

Sessions run 60 minutes with a clear structure designed to maximize touches on the ball and build real skills — not just burn energy.

01

Warm-Up & Ball Mastery ~10 min

Individual ball work to get every player comfortable and focused. Moves, juggling, first touch.

02

Technical Skill Block ~20 min

The focus of the day — passing, shooting, 1v1 defense, receiving, finishing. Coached with clear instruction and repetition.

03

Small-Sided Games ~20 min

Applying the skill in competition. Small groups create more touches, more decisions, and more game-like scenarios than 11v11 ever could.

04

Full Scrimmage & Debrief ~10 min

Players put everything together in a real game. Coach gives individual feedback. Kids leave knowing exactly what to work on next.

Parent FAQ

Questions parents ask before enrolling.

What ages is this for? ▼
Group classes are open to kids in Kindergarten through 8th grade. Players are grouped by age and ability so every child is appropriately challenged. Private training is available for any age.
Does my child need prior experience? ▼
No. Beginners are absolutely welcome. Qumtak is designed to build fundamentals from the ground up. If your child has never kicked a ball in a structured setting, this is a great starting point.
Where are classes held? ▼
Spring 2026 group classes run at Sanborn Elementary in Andover, MA. Private training locations are flexible — contact us to arrange a session near you. Reading-area sessions are coming soon.
How much does it cost? ▼
Spring 2026 group classes are $260 for Andover residents and $275 for non-residents — covering the full session (Apr 27–Jun 12, Monday and Friday 5:50–6:50 PM). Private training rates are available upon inquiry.
Are there tryouts? Can any child join? ▼
No tryouts. Ever. Qumtak is open enrollment — any child in the age range can sign up. We believe elite coaching should be accessible to every kid, regardless of current skill level.
How do I register? ▼
Group classes in Andover are registered through the Andover Recreation Department on MyRec. Click the "Enroll Now" button on this page. For private training, book directly through the link above or contact Coach Shaya at noah@qmtk.org.
What should my child bring to sessions? ▼
Soccer cleats or athletic shoes, shin guards, a ball (if they have one — extras are available), and water. Dress for the weather — sessions run rain or shine unless fields are unsafe.
Is Qumtak coming to Reading? ▼
Yes. Qumtak is actively working to launch sessions in Reading. If you're a Reading parent interested in classes, or a Reading Recreation official interested in a partnership, reach out at noah@qmtk.org.
